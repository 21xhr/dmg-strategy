# Internal Organization Use Cases

## Purpose

This document extracts the internal-organization and HR-adjacent ideas from the brainstorm source.

This is the best current interpretation of what the former `INTERNAL_TEAM` tenant idea was trying to capture.

## Better tenant-type framing

If the tenant type is meant to describe the business or operator context, `INTERNAL_ORGANIZATION` is a better label than `INTERNAL_TEAM`.

Why:

- the product may be used across an entire company, not one team
- it may fit institutions, associations, or departments
- the value proposition is governance and alignment, not only team collaboration

## Core value proposition

In a normal hierarchy, a direct manager can suppress, distort, or simply fail to surface the real intensity of a team concern.

DMG changes that by making collective commitment visible.

The system turns:

- hidden friction
- blocked ideas
- diffuse dissatisfaction

into quantifiable organizational signals.

## Example: N+1 / N+2 conflict

In a classic structure, an N+1 can ignore a concern without the N+2 seeing how serious it really is.

With DMG:

- the intensity of employee commitment becomes measurable
- the disagreement becomes visible rather than anecdotal
- the N+2 can arbitrate on documented priority rather than rumor

This does not remove hierarchy.

It gives hierarchy a better signal.

## Internal buyer angles

Three good framings for this market:

### Governance and transparency

- official rule-of-the-game layer for prioritization
- reduction of perceived arbitrariness
- clearer legitimacy of decisions

### Operational arbitration

- expose priority conflicts early
- reveal misalignment between base and management
- support explicit tradeoff decisions instead of informal pressure

### Organizational health

- detect recurring frustration before churn
- identify constructive silent contributors
- make employee agency tangible instead of symbolic

## Why this is strategically interesting

This vertical may later connect to portable identity and influence portability.

A person who proves constructive influence in one context may carry a stronger starting trust signal into another context.

That capability should be treated carefully and consensually, and it should remain optional upside rather than the first product impression.
