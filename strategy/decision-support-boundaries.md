# Decision Support Boundaries

## Purpose

This note defines a small but important product-positioning boundary: structuring inputs, synthesizing them, recommending actions, and holding final decision authority are not the same layer.

## Core distinction

DMG can be discussed across four layers:

- structured input capture
- summarization and synthesis
- recommendation or option generation
- final decision authority

These layers should not collapse into one rhetorical block.

## Positioning rule

When the audience is sensitive to delegated judgment, present DMG first as infrastructure that improves how inputs are formed, carried, and understood.

Do not imply that better decision support automatically means delegated decision authority.

## First signal from AAJD

The AAJD conversation strengthened this boundary.

The reaction was positive to note-taking, synthesis, and workflow acceleration, but negative to the idea that AI analysis would become the real decision-maker.

That makes the separation commercially important, not only philosophically neat.
