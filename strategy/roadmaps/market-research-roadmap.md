# Market Research Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines a durable, multi-phase market-research program for DMG.

It exists to turn scattered research prompts, one-off deep-search outputs, and partial competitor notes into a repeatable system for:

- competition mapping
- market trend tracking
- architecture research
- SWOT support
- thesis validation across multiple wedges

The initial source material for this roadmap lives in `strategy/market-analysis/market-researches/2026-04-27-market-research-program-notes.md`.

See the [roadmap index](../roadmap.md) for the active roadmap list.

## Completion criteria

This roadmap can be considered complete when these conditions are true:

- one stable market-research workflow exists from broad scan to focused deep dives
- one durable competition watchlist exists and separates direct competitors from adjacent tools
- one reusable DSS architecture comparison table exists and is maintained in present-state language
- one explicit method exists for linking sourced market evidence into SWOT updates
- one clear rule exists for when new research belongs in backlog, in a roadmap, in a competitor note, or in a reusable market-analysis note

## Architecture to preserve

The research system should preserve these boundaries:

- broad scans establish taxonomy and keyword maps
- deep dives inspect one architecture, category, or regulatory surface at a time
- competitor notes remain company- or product-specific
- reusable market notes stay at category or architecture level
- SWOT updates consume evidence rather than becoming the first place where raw research is stored

## Target outcome

The target is a research program that produces durable outputs instead of ad hoc accumulation.

That includes:

- a taxonomy-first search cadence
- scoped deep-dive prompts and output shapes
- a watchlist for direct and adjacent competition
- architecture comparison notes that can feed strategy decisions
- explicit reuse of findings in competitor analysis, wedge selection, and fundraising support material

## Migration phases

### Phase 1 - Taxonomy foundation

Status: completed on 2026-04-30

Goal:

- establish a stable classification map before chasing individual tools or trends

Deliverables:

- one broad taxonomy note for DSS evolution through 2026
- one classification table covering data-driven, model-driven, knowledge-driven, communication-driven, and agentic branches
- one glossary review pass for recurring market-research terms introduced by this work

Checkpoint signals:

- the same category names can be reused consistently across later competitor notes
- future deep searches have clear keywords and scope boundaries

### Phase 2 - Competition watchlist

Status: completed on 2026-04-30

Goal:

- separate direct competitors from adjacent categories that only overlap partially with DMG

Deliverables:

- one watchlist note grouping tools by direct competition, adjacent collaboration, knowledge systems, and analytic intelligence
- one rule set for what counts as direct versus adjacent overlap
- one first pass on buyer, integration, and governance differences between categories

Checkpoint signals:

- new tools can be added without rethinking the whole map each time
- the watchlist can support both strategy conversations and fundraising positioning

### Phase 3 - Architecture deep dives

Status: completed on 2026-04-30

Goal:

- inspect the most strategically relevant architecture families with focused research rather than one blended note

Deliverables:

- one deep-dive note per architecture family judged strategically relevant
- prompt templates for broad scan, architecture dive, and regulatory dive
- one cross-architecture comparison table covering deciding mechanism, integration burden, governance posture, and human-in-the-loop boundary

Checkpoint signals:

- each deep dive sharpens either product-boundary thinking, competitor mapping, or go-to-market language
- duplicate or redundant research paths are easier to reject early

### Phase 4 - Regulatory and governance overlay

Status: completed on 2026-04-30

Goal:

- integrate auditability, human oversight, and regulatory constraints into the market map rather than treat them as an afterthought

Deliverables:

- one regulatory overlay note for EU AI Act and adjacent governance constraints relevant to DSS-style systems
- one reusable checklist of governance questions for competitor and architecture review
- one integration point from governance constraints into competitor notes where relevant

Checkpoint signals:

- high-risk versus low-risk categories are easier to distinguish in strategic discussions
- governance claims in future notes become more specific and source-backed

### Phase 5 - SWOT and strategy integration

Status: completed on 2026-04-30

Goal:

- make research outputs reusable in strategy documents instead of leaving them isolated in research folders

Deliverables:

- one repeatable rule for when research changes SWOT inputs
- one update pass on relevant strategy notes once the first structured outputs exist
- one lightweight evidence-linking pattern so future strategy claims point back to the research source material

Checkpoint signals:

- SWOT updates cite actual research notes instead of memory or inference alone
- market-research work produces visible downstream value in strategy decisions

## Risks to control

- blending direct competitors and adjacent tools into one flat list
- jumping into deep dives before the taxonomy is stable enough to guide them
- storing raw research directly inside SWOT-style notes
- letting one-off prompts create output sprawl without durable placement rules
- mixing architecture analysis, company analysis, and thesis claims inside the same note without explicit boundaries

## Exit review questions

- Can a new tool or company be placed quickly into the correct category?
- Can a new deep-search result be routed to the right durable note without reopening the whole system?
- Can a future SWOT update point to concrete research outputs?
- Does the roadmap leave behind a reusable research operating model rather than a one-time document set?
