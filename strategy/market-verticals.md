# Market Verticals

## Purpose

This document extracts the main market surfaces currently implied by the brainstorm material.

These are not separate products.

They are vertical applications of one shared DMG engine.

## Streaming

Streaming remains the fastest validation loop.

Why it matters first:

- fastest feedback cycle
- fastest pricing tests
- fastest retention signal
- easiest visible proof that the system changes live prioritization behavior

Streaming is the current wedge, not the final limit of the product.

## SaaS

The SaaS opportunity is structured product-roadmap arbitration.

Instead of a soft voting board, customers allocate limited influence toward features they care about most.

That creates a more credible prioritization signal than low-cost voting tools.

DMG adds:

- scarcity
- opportunity cost
- strategic ranking
- measurable customer conviction

## Service businesses

Service businesses with constrained capacity are a strong fit because they constantly arbitrate competing demands.

Core value:

- turn queue frustration into structured prioritization
- make tradeoffs legible to customers
- protect the operator from accusations of arbitrariness

Example fits:

- artisan businesses
- renovation and building companies
- premium service operations with bottlenecks

## Internal organizations

Internal organization use cases are not about external customers.

They are about governance, alignment, and visibility inside a company, institution, association, or structured group.

This vertical deserves its own dedicated framing because the buyer, the users, and the political value of the product are different from streaming or customer-service cases.

See [[internal-organization-use-cases]].

## Cross-vertical common pain

Across SaaS, service businesses, and internal organizations, one recurring pain is not only arbitration.

It is delayed understanding.

See [[uncertainty-reduction]].

That is why DMG is not only a prioritization tool.

It is also a system for reducing uncertainty before committing execution capacity.

## Strategic rule

These verticals should be treated as packaging layers around one engine.

The product should not fragment into one separate schema and one separate codebase per market.
