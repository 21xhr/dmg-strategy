# Airtable

Snapshot date: 2026-04-30

## Why it matters

Airtable is increasingly used as a flexible decision-support workspace where teams combine data structuring, scoring, and workflow automation.

It can look adjacent to DMG when teams use scoring systems for prioritization.

## Evidence signal to track

The Airtable article "How an Airtable scoring system helps you make more confident decisions" signals that users are actively shaping weighted decision logic in Airtable.

Working link from current research thread:

- https://blog.airtable.com/how-an-airtable-scoring-system-helps-you-make-more-confident-decisions/

## Overlap with DMG

Real overlap:

- weighted scoring for prioritization
- collaborative review around structured inputs
- configurable workflow around decision candidates

Boundary where DMG remains differentiated:

- formal arbitration when legitimate priorities conflict across stakeholders
- reusable historical decision rationale, not only current scoring state
- governance-grade legitimacy controls (contestability, authority, and explicit justification)

## Current classification

Adjacent workflow and scoring platform, not yet a direct strategic-arbitration competitor under the current direct-vs-adjacent rule.

## What to verify next

- whether Airtable native patterns now include first-class arbitration history reuse rather than static score snapshots
- whether role-based contestability and decision legitimacy are supported as product boundaries
- whether Airtable is sold in practice as strategic governance infrastructure or as flexible operational coordination

## Implication for DMG narrative

Airtable should be used as a precision comparison point:

- Airtable demonstrates why teams adopt configurable scoring quickly.
- DMG demonstrates why scoring alone is insufficient when arbitration legitimacy and reusable decision memory become core requirements.
