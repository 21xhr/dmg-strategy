# Open Source Politics

## Why this file matters

Open Source Politics matters because it represents the service and implementation layer around Decidim in France more clearly than Decidim alone.

It is useful for DMG because it shows where a meaningful share of the value, risk, and continuity burden can sit when democratic infrastructure is open-source but not self-sufficient in deployment.

## What appears to have been true

Public institutional platforms show Open Source Politics as the builder behind the French Senate petitions platform and the French National Assembly petitions platform, both using Decidim.

That makes OSP strategically important even if it was not the software originator.

Its role appears to have included:

- implementation
- adaptation to institutional requirements
- deployment support
- practical continuity for live democratic platforms

## Why the 2026 acquisition matters

In April 2026, the Decidim Association publicly stated that Open Source Politics had been acquired by Go Vocal after financial difficulties over several years.

That statement matters because it reveals a real fragility in the service-provider layer.

The software may be open.

But the institutions running it can still become operationally dependent on a small number of specialist implementers.

When one of those implementers weakens financially or changes ownership, continuity risk becomes visible immediately.

## Strategic lesson for DMG

Open Source Politics is useful because it reveals that infrastructure economics do not stop at the software layer.

They also sit in:

- integration labor
- governance design
- maintenance capacity
- institutional trust
- continuity of the service provider

That is directly relevant for DMG.

If DMG becomes governance infrastructure, the product will also need a credible answer to the service-layer question:

- who implements it
- who maintains it
- who owns the client relationship
- what happens if the integrator weakens or disappears

## Practical implication

The useful comparison is not only:

- open versus closed

It is also:

- packaged vendor software with integrated support
- open or modular infrastructure that still depends on a fragile implementation ecosystem

That is why OSP matters as a competitor-adjacent note rather than only as a historical footnote inside Decidim.
