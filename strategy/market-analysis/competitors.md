# Competitors

## Purpose

This file maps the main competitor categories and links to deeper notes on individual companies or products.

## Categories

## What market analysis should inspect

Each market should be checked not only for willingness to pay, but for the governance surfaces it already exposes.

The useful questions are:

- what prioritization or arbitration surface already exists
- whether weighting is explicit or hidden inside status, loudness, free time, or admin discretion
- whether the current workflow already has approval, moderation, queueing, escalation, or budget-allocation logic
- whether existing governance surfaces are strong enough for the real decision burden or too generic to be trusted
- whether DMG would replace a surface, strengthen a weak one, or add a missing one

### Product feedback and roadmap tools

- Canny
- Upvoty
- Productboard
- Productroad-style tools

Why they matter:

- they prove roadmap arbitration and customer-prioritization workflow are real buying categories

### Live interaction and creator tooling

- Twitch Polls
- native chat-command layers
- Streamlabs-style tooling

Why they matter:

- they prove live operators pay for interaction tooling, even if the governance depth is limited

### Broad polling and consultation tools

- Doodle
- Typeform
- Slido

Why they matter:

- they prove low-friction input collection is already normalized across many organizations

### DAO governance and weighted allocation tools

- Snapshot
- Tally
- Aragon
- Gitcoin

Why they matter:

- they prove weighted governance and quadratic-style allocation concepts are already productized somewhere, even if in different environments

### Civitech and participation infrastructure

- Make.org
- Decidim
- Go Vocal
- Open Source Politics
- Make.org-like consultation suites more broadly

Why they matter:

- they prove institutions and organizations already buy large-scale consultation, participation, and transformation-alignment tools
- many of them compete by filtering and synthesizing mass expression after the interaction happens, which sharpens DMG's differentiation around structuring interaction rules upstream
- they are mostly environment-bounded around civic, public-sector, and institutional use cases, so the cross-vertical lesson should be extracted carefully rather than assumed automatically
