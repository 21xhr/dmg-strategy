# Competitive Positioning

## Purpose

This document is the messaging layer for competition, not the full research inventory.

The goal is not to claim DMG has no competition.

The goal is to explain how to talk about overlap and differentiation without turning every positioning doc into a competitor catalog.

For the category map and company-specific research notes, see [[strategy/market-analysis/competitors]].

## Positioning rule

DMG competes against adjacent categories more than against one exact clone.

That is the credible framing.

Say:

- existing tools solve slices of the problem
- DMG combines structured governance, scarce influence, and operational arbitration in one system

Do not say:

- there is no competition
- DMG is just Twitch polls but better

## What overlap actually means

DMG overlaps with several categories, but not in the same way.

- with feedback tools, the overlap is structured demand capture
- with live interaction tools, the overlap is low-friction participation
- with polling or consultation tools, the overlap is input collection and theme emergence
- with DAO or quadratic systems, the overlap is weighted influence and formalized allocation rules
- with civic or transformation platforms, the overlap is large-scale structured participation before action

That is enough to show the market is real.

The point is not that DMG fully matches any one of those products.

The point is that DMG sits at the intersection of several already-legible categories.

## Where DMG is stronger

- DMG is strongest when the real problem is deciding what actually gets done when too many requests compete for limited delivery capacity, time, money, or attention, not just collecting opinions or expressions of interest.
- DMG is designed for recurring operational decisions rather than one-off consultation moments.
- DMG can combine endogenous and exogenous influence sources depending on the tenant philosophy, instead of forcing one universal model.
- DMG sits closer to the execution loop, where limited time, money, attention, or delivery capacity make prioritization economically painful.

- this file explains the framing

## Useful short comparison

DMG is not a wishlist board.

DMG is not a toy poll.

DMG is not only a governance theory demo.

DMG is a decision governance infrastructure for contexts where too many requests compete for limited time, attention, money, or execution capacity.

## Strongest cross-vertical market signal

- organizations already pay for tools that collect structured demand
- organizations already pay for consultation and participation products
- organizations already spend time and money trying to reduce decision noise and shorten feedback loops

That is why the credible argument is not only about streaming interaction.

It is that many environments already pay to make messy demand more legible, and DMG becomes relevant when that demand must be arbitrated under real constraints.

## Practical pitch line

Existing competitors generally help collect opinions, feedback, or engagement.

DMG is stronger when the real problem is deciding what gets priority when execution capacity is limited.
