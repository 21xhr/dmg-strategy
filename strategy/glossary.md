# Strategy Glossary

## Purpose

This note holds private reminder entries that do not belong in the shareable glossary.

## Terms

- API contracts: What data an API exposes and the rules governing how that data can be accessed, updated, or manipulated. This keeps each surface behavior and data boundary explicit so policy boundaries do not bleed together.

## Product and business terms

- DMG: Decision Making Governance (subject to change)
- MVP: Minimum Viable Product
- PMF: Product Market Fit
- LOI: Letter of Intent
- ARR: Annual Recurring Revenue
- MRR: Monthly Recurring Revenue
- B2B: business-to-business
- B2C: business-to-consumer
- B2B2C: business-to-business-to-consumer
- TAM: Total Addressable Market
- CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost
- LTV: Lifetime Value
- pre-money valuation: the company's assessed value before investment money comes in
- dilution: the percentage of ownership given up when issuing shares to investors
- acqui-hire: an acquisition where the buyer mainly wants the team rather than the product

## Technical terms

- API: Application Programming Interface
- PR: Pull Request, a proposed set of repository changes submitted for review before merging into the main branch
- CI: Continuous Integration, the automated build, test, validation, and artifact-check pipeline that runs when code changes are pushed or proposed
- JWT: JSON Web Token
- TTL: Time To Live, the maximum lifetime before a token, cache entry, or other scoped artifact expires automatically
- UTC: Coordinated Universal Time
- UI: User Interface
- UX: User Experience
- SLO: Service Level Objective, a defined performance target such as uptime
- quadratic voting: a voting mechanism where each additional vote costs more than the previous one, reducing domination by a single actor

## Strategy and market terms

- vertical: a specific market segment or industry with its own users, constraints, buying logic, and use cases
- wedge: the first narrow market entry point used to validate adoption before expanding more broadly
- moat: a defensible competitive advantage that makes a product harder to displace
- pilot: a limited real-world deployment with one customer or user group, one clear use case, and explicit success criteria

## Organizational and sector terms

- HR: Human Resources
- DG: Directeur general, equivalent to general management or director-general level depending on context
- CA: Conseil d'Administration, the governing board of an association or similar organization
- CSE: Comite Social et Economique, the elected staff-representation body consulted on defined employment and workplace matters
- ARS: Agence Regionale de Sante, the French regional health authority
- IME: Institut Medico-Educatif
- ITEP: Institut Therapeutique, Educatif et Pedagogique
- EHPAD: Etablissement d'Hebergement pour Personnes Agees Dependantes
- ESAT: Etablissement et Service d'Aide par le Travail
- HAS: Haute Autorite de Sante
- DUERP: Document Unique d'Evaluation des Risques Professionnels, the mandatory workplace risk-evaluation document
- PCA: Plan de Continuite d'Activite, the continuity-planning document for maintaining service during disruption
- SDI: Schema Directeur des Systemes d'Information, a strategic information-systems framework used to organize objectives, priorities, and system evolution
- codir: comite de direction, the internal leadership group where cross-cutting operational and strategic issues are escalated
- GEPP: Groupe d'Evaluation et de Pilotage Pluriel, used in the AAJD notes for plural evaluation and steering groups that help surface recurring issues and systemic questions
- AGEVAL: medico-social software used in the AAJD notes as an operational base for tracking objectives, progress, and field information

## Organizations mentioned in current outreach notes

- AAJD: Association pour l'Aide aux Adultes et aux Jeunes en Difficulte, a French nonprofit association working in social and medico-social support contexts
- APAEIA: Association des Parents et Amis d'Enfants & Adultes Inadaptes de l'Avranchin, services personnalises a destination des enfants et adultes en situation de handicap ou fragilises
- UNAPEI: Union Nationale des Associations de Parents d'Enfants Inadaptes
- OSP: Open Source Politics, French civic-tech implementation company historically associated with Decidim deployments for public institutions and acquired by Go Vocal in March 2026
