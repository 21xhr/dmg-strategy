# Audience Pitch Notes

## Purpose

This note extracts the reusable audience-specific pitch angles from the brainstorm inbox.

Use it as a working companion to the main advisor brief, not as a replacement for it.

## Founder baseline

Reusable founder story:

- builder profile with visible product discipline
- Quarkois experience as prior evidence of coordination work
- resilience and constraint turned into product discipline
- focus on reducing uncertainty and removing noise

Useful founder line:

DMG was built from a real need to structure overwhelming interaction.

## Marc or Jim style conversation

Primary posture:

- ask for advice first
- show product, thesis, and gaps honestly
- ask for critique, introductions, and pressure-testing

See [[fundraising/briefs/advisor-brief-marc-jim]] for the main version.

## Public-Investment-Bank Style Conversation

Best angle:

- founder seriousness
- local legitimacy
- structured innovation story
- clear use of funds and execution discipline

Good emphasis:

- documented product logic already exists
- the company is moving from functional MVP toward reproducible deployment and pilots
- the project is legible as innovation and coordination infrastructure, not as a hobby tool

## Glaz Or Traditional Operator Conversation

Best angle:

- cost of interruption and feedback lag
- protection of decision-maker time
- reduction of uncertainty before expensive execution choices
- governance clarity under pressure
- proof of reliability from prior working relationships

Good language:

- decision support
- operational sovereignty
- customer visibility
- feedback compression
- arbitration method
- coordination engine

Avoid leading with:

- SaaS jargon
- Web3 framing
- abstract platform rhetoric

Cost of interruption in this context:

- time lost because leaders are forced to arbitrate noise before acting
- execution slowed by low-quality urgent requests
- expensive work started with weak customer or stakeholder signal

For groups like Glaz, Serafel, or Keralia, another strong angle is not only arbitration but uncertainty reduction.

The underlying pain is often that the company is navigating with delayed, fragmented, or low-resolution feedback.

That creates:

- products shaped too far from the client reality
- slow feedback loops on adoption problems
- overreliance on partial financial indicators
- weak confidence in where to push next

DMG can be framed there as a way to shorten the distance between base reality and executive decision.

It helps turn scattered demand, weak signals, and lagging reactions into a clearer operating picture.

See [[uncertainty-reduction]] for the longer strategy framing.

## Streamer conversation

Best angle:

- remove chaos from chat
- let the audience participate without losing operator control
- make the final decision easier to defend

Good demo pattern:

- seed a small set of options
- give viewers a starting balance
- let the audience experience the quadratic cost directly
- show that the animation runs without constant manual intervention by the streamer

## Practical reminder

Different audiences do not need different product truths.

They need different entry points into the same product truth.
