# Investor Sequencing Note

## Purpose

Internal rehearsal memo for the sequencing story.

Use it to keep the infrastructure narrative disciplined before it is compressed into deck or spoken fundraising language.

For a shorter outward-facing derivative, see [[fundraising/one-pagers/dmg-investor-deck-language]].

## Core sequencing

The route is:

1. streaming wedge
2. first service-company wedge
3. broader infrastructure expansion

## Step 1: Streaming wedge

The streaming wedge is not the whole company.

It is the fastest environment to prove that structuring behavior upstream has real consequences on operator governance, repeated use, retention, and low-friction participation.

The key proof points are:

- the mechanism changes behavior in real time
- the operator gets clearer governance signal than with generic polling
- the audience can use it repeatedly without heavy onboarding
- the system works under live pressure rather than only in theory

## Step 2: First service-company wedge

The next step is not to generalize to every market at once.

It is to prove that the same governance logic matters in a B2B environment where execution capacity is scarce and prioritization pain is economically real.

The key proof points are:

- the system can plug into an existing operating workflow
- the tenant can configure meaningful rule logic
- the output is more decision-usable than generic feedback tooling
- integration and support work remain bounded enough to repeat

## Step 3: Broader infrastructure expansion

Only after those proofs does the broader infrastructure story become credible.

That is where DMG can start to look less like a wedge product and more like a governance layer deployable across multiple operational contexts.

The expansion thesis is stronger when the company can already show:

- one high-frequency wedge proving the mechanism under pressure
- one non-streaming wedge proving the logic transfers into operational environments
- a repeatable enough delivery model to support broader scale

## Why this sequencing matters

A credible investor story does not require the final company form to exist on day one.

They need to believe the route toward it is disciplined.

That means showing:

- awareness of what governance infrastructure actually requires
- clear packaging work from personal product toward SaaS readiness
- a first wedge that is small enough to prove execution without carrying the full infrastructure burden too early

## Revenue implication

The streaming wedge does not need to start with broad paid adoption to be useful.

But the broader company story gets much stronger once the product reaches a SaaS-ready state and at least one wedge begins producing paid usage or paid pilot behavior.

Paid proof matters because it shows the mechanism is not only interesting in a demo environment.
