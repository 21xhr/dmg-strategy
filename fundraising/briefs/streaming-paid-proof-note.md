# Streaming Paid-Proof Note

## Purpose

This note defines what should count as meaningful pilot evidence in the streaming wedge before DMG tries to carry a broader institutional or infrastructure story.

## Core rule

The streaming wedge does not need to prove the whole company.

It needs to prove that upstream behavior structuring produces repeatable governance value under real usage.

## What meaningful pilot evidence looks like

Meaningful pilot evidence in streaming should show most of the following:

- repeated use across multiple sessions rather than one novelty spike
- operator willingness to rely on the system for real audience arbitration rather than only for demo moments
- audience participation that stays active without heavy explanation every session
- signs of retention, such as returning users or repeated paid or earned influence use
- evidence that the system changes operator governance, not just audience entertainment
- low enough friction that the mechanism can run inside normal stream flow without constant operator rescue

## What paid proof means here

The best version is paid usage from the streaming wedge once the product reaches a credible SaaS form.

That does not require a large customer base immediately.

It does require at least one environment where the operator is paying for the system because it creates real governance value rather than because it is an interesting experiment.

## Why this matters before institutional expansion

Institutional and B2B expansion becomes more believable when the first wedge has already shown:

- repeated use
- behavioral effect
- governance value
- willingness to pay or equivalent economic commitment

Without that proof, the broader infrastructure story can still sound elegant but premature.
