# DMG Advisor Brief

## Purpose
This note is for trusted operators or advisors who can help pressure-test the company thesis, sharpen priorities, and identify the best next strategic moves.

## What DMG Is
DMG is a decision governance infrastructure.

It started as a livestreaming product: a system that lets a community submit, promote, and prioritize actions or ideas through a structured influence model rather than chaotic chat noise.

Its core differentiator is a quadratic-style influence logic combined with lifecycle rules, scarcity, and transparent arbitration.

The broader thesis is that this engine is not limited to streaming. It can be applied anywhere a decision-maker faces too many requests, limited time or resources, and noisy demand.

## Current State
- Working MVP exists today.
- Backend and frontend are implemented.
- Product logic is documented in depth.
- Current launch architecture uses Lumia for chat-command ingress and points authority.
- Product is demoable in streaming context.
- Founder is now evaluating the move from personal MVP to company-building mode.

## Why This Might Matter
DMG is not being framed as "a better poll".
It is being framed as a governance layer for resolving real-time priority conflicts.

Examples:
- streaming: viewers influence what gets played or executed
- SaaS: users influence feature prioritization through structured cost and support
- artisan / service businesses: clients influence priority slots through transparent rules
- internal teams: stakeholders surface structured priority instead of informal noise

## Why This Is Early but Serious
What exists already:
- real product logic
- a differentiated governance mechanism
- a credible streaming wedge
- architecture and implementation depth
- unusually strong documentation for this stage

What does not yet exist:
- meaningful external traction
- paid pilots
- SaaS packaging
- team beyond founder

## What I Am Trying To Validate
I am trying to answer four practical questions:

1. Is the strongest first wedge still streaming, given the MVP is already closest to usable there?
2. What is the best path from this personal MVP to a SaaS-ready product without overbuilding too early?
3. What would make this company legible and credible to early investors?
4. Which early market or pilot path would create the strongest validation signal?
