# DMG Investor Deck Language

## Purpose

Short fundraising derivative for deck copy, meeting openings, and concise written investor material.

Use this after the fuller one-pager and sequencing memo have already made the logic clear.

## Core framing

DMG is a governance infrastructure layer for situations where too many requests compete for limited execution capacity.

The wedge starts in streaming because streaming is the fastest environment to prove that better rule design changes behavior under pressure.

Expansion follows only after that proof transfers into a first operational wedge where prioritization pain is economically real.

## Three-line deck version

- streaming proves the mechanism under pressure
- a first service-company wedge proves the logic transfers beyond streaming
- broader infrastructure expansion follows only after repeatable proof, not before it

## Adoption and moat

DMG does not require a rip-and-replace deployment.

The stronger adoption story is a non-invasive governance layer that plugs into existing systems and strengthens governance surfaces that are weak, missing, or too generic for the decision burden.

That matters because the moat is not only algorithmic. It also sits in integration fit, support credibility, and continuity control.

## Founder-readiness framing

The founder does not need to look like the final operator of a mature infrastructure company on day one.

The burden is to show disciplined sequencing, clear awareness of what the larger company would require, and real proof at the current wedge.

## Investor close

DMG should be read as a governance-infrastructure thesis with a narrow proof sequence, not as a small interaction feature stretching for a larger story.
