## Durable lines worth reusing

- Le désordre n'est pas qu'un inconfort, c'est une faute de gestion subie.
- On vend du temps de cerveau disponible.
- DMG n'est pas un gadget de stream, c'est une infrastructure de gouvernance décisionnelle.
- Les systèmes actuels mesurent surtout le bruit; DMG cherche à rendre visible l'engagement réel.
- DMG is not only a prioritization tool. It is also a system for reducing uncertainty before committing execution capacity.
- The stronger cross-vertical framing is not only that streaming needs better live interaction. It is that many environments already spend money trying to reduce decision noise, shorten feedback loops, and turn diffuse demand into a more actionable priority signal.
- DMG is stronger when the real problem is deciding what gets priority when execution capacity is limited.
- Workflows can automate execution flow; DMG captures value where organizations must arbitrate between competing priorities, formalize why, and retain reusable decision history.
- DMG is stronger when it is framed as a system for designing behavior through explicit rules.
- Many competitors compete by filtering and synthesizing mass expression after the interaction happens, while DMG structures interaction rules upstream.
- DMG's stronger hypothesis is that you design the interaction rules upstream so meaningful behavior is generated before analysis.
- Serious consultation platforms also structure interaction upstream to some extent.
- DMG can be framed as participation infrastructure that inserts clients, users, or communities into operational flows more legibly than generic engagement tools do.
- DMG is a decision-governance layer that can sit on top of existing customer and operational flows.
- DMG is easier to position when framed first as infrastructure for clearer inputs and explicit decision boundaries, not as a machine that replaces decision-makers.
- **DMG positioning has distinct layers**: structured input capture, summarization and synthesis, recommendation, and final decision authority should be presented separately rather than collapsed into one promise.
    - AI-assisted upstream input structuring is a capability inside the structured-input layer, not the whole product promise.
    - Named document types and existing synthesis habits are not the same thing as strongly structured, comparable, decision-ready upstream data.
- We do not invent document structure. We help define and enforce the decision-usable shape inside real existing flows.
- DMG is for the decision body first, but the quality of employee-side input still matters because it determines the quality of the signal the managers receive.
- The point is not to say that IMAGO fails to structure inputs; the point is to make upstream decision structure a spearhead of DMG itself.
- “why investing in structure first can still be rational”: The thesis is hard rhetorically, but economically it is not strange at all. Firms already buy ERP, CRM, workflow systems, approval systems, ticketing systems, and compliance systems because **upstream order creates downstream advantage**.
- Like ERP, it can create more dependable downstream decisions by enforcing more disciplined upstream inputs and rules.
- **Scaling cost**: if the rule architecture is good, cost should scale more slowly than participation volume because less human moderation, interpretation, and manual synthesis is needed per additional unit of participation.
- DMG is about gathering more decision-usable inputs, not only more inputs.
- The decision-maker sees not only what people say they want, but what different participants are prepared to support under explicit rules.
- DMG isn't “just a highly customized tool” but a configurable governance architecture with tenant-specific rule design.
- **DMG becomes infrastructure for legible collective decision-making under tenant-specific rules**
- A durable product line to test repeatedly: tenant-scoped governance workflows plus tenant-scoped governance event history, with reusable workflow modules activated per tenant profile.
- **Is DMG less democratic?** DMG can make weighting explicit instead of letting loudness, free time, or raw activity become the hidden weighting system.
- DMG aims to structure how influence is earned, constrained, and spent so the **resulting behavior is more decision-grade** under real execution limits.
- The stronger product hypothesis is to design system rules that generate decision-grade participation upstream.
- **Richer influence allocation** transforms simple opinion into a more decision-usable signal by attaching clearer structure, weighting, and tradeoffs at the moment the signal is created.
- **DMG does not need to replace the tenant stack**. It can branch onto existing systems and design how influence is earned and spent from signals the tenant already has.
- Infrastructure defensibility is not a euphemism for lock-in. The stronger claim is that once a system fits real workflows and decision rules, retention becomes structurally higher.
- The streaming wedge becomes valuable if it proves upstream rule design changes behavior, improves operator governance, creates repeated use, and does so with low friction. 

## Open ideas still worth pressure-testing

### Integrity and verified influence

- humanity proofs, KYC tiers, or zk-style proofs as future anti-astroturfing layers
- behavioral detection such as a "signature d'hésitation" for suspiciously optimal influence patterns
- positioning for high-trust or institutional environments without overselling the current product state

### Long-term scalability and control

- when it becomes economically rational to internalize the points engine
- when to build in-house listeners instead of staying at the adapter boundary
- what realtime architecture is required when usage goes far beyond the current MVP scale

### Participation design

- delegated influence or liquid-democracy-style participation
- thematic specialization of influence
- how much complexity can be hidden while keeping the system legible to normal users

### Next vertical experiments

- EdTech and distance-learning participation
- civic or media participation surfaces
- strongest non-streaming pilot after streaming
