# Current Network Action Sheet

## Purpose

This table is the operational follow-up layer for the current network.

Use it to track priority, access quality, primary motion, next contact step, and the concrete signal to extract.

For relationship context, see [[external-network/network-contacts/current-network]].

## Working rule

Each row should stay action-oriented.

If a conversation produces deeper notes, keep those elsewhere and only update the fields that change the next move.

The tag columns are intentionally not exhaustive descriptions of the whole relationship.

They are there to force one primary motion, one access type, and a small number of secondary gains so the table stays operational.

## Tag legend

### Access

- `warm`: good existing relationship
- `very-warm`: unusually strong personal or family trust
- `bridge`: access depends on an intermediary
- `public`: relationship is real, but the contact sits in a more public or institutional role

### Primary motion

- `pilot-fit`: test whether a concrete pilot path exists
- `pain-map`: problem discovery focused on real operating pain, current workaround, and where the signal is currently weak
- `language-test`: pressure-test wording and market legibility
- `critique`: stress-test strategy, wedge, or sequencing, meaning the order of moves such as who to talk to first, which market to validate first, and when to switch from discovery to pilot-selling
- `institutional-fit`: test whether the framing works in public, civic, or institutional settings

### Secondary gains

- `repeat-use`: check whether repeat usage is realistic
- `owner-fit`: identify who could actually own the next step internally
- `budget-fit`: understand what spending logic could justify the product
- `internal-fit`: test whether internal governance, communication, or employee-input problems are a stronger entry point than customer-facing prioritization
- `referral`: see whether the contact can open other doors
- `credibility`: increase confidence that the thesis is serious in that environment
- `language`: extract sharper operator wording
