## Hybrid management model

[Inferential] The AAJD model is not described as a heavy bureaucracy with many layers of chiefs and sub-chiefs. Stan instead describes a hybrid structure: someone still arbitrates and bears decision risk, but the organization also operates through horizontal and matrix-style problem surfacing.

[Inferential] He compares this horizontal layer to Japanese quality circles, where operators closest to the work surface defects, recurring friction, and corrective ideas instead of leaving diagnosis only to top-level designers.

## GEPP groups and transversal escalation

[Inferential] He describes GEPP groups as plural evaluation and piloting groups built inside each dispositif. Directors can address some issues directly there, then escalate systemic or cross-dispositif issues into codir when they exceed one local unit.

[Inferential] His decision logic is collegial rather than solitary. He may begin with a firm view, but discussion with colleagues can change the decision if field operators demonstrate that a spend or adjustment is actually justified.

## DUERP example and role diversity

[Inferential] For a psychosocial-risk evaluation, the GEPP should not be mono-profession. It should include several real job types from the dispositif, such as a driver, house manager, secretary, educator, psychologist, and manager, so that the risk map reflects the actual diversity of work situations.

[Inferential] The same young people can create very different operational risk depending on the role and moment. A driver alone with nine young people during transport is not in the same situation as a house manager supervising mealtime. The point is not to solve everything at once, but to make those realities legible inside the mandatory risk-evaluation process.

## Cross-dispositif pattern detection

[Inferential] Stan's stronger point is that similar issues can recur across ITEP, IME, and other dispositifs. When the same transport or isolation problem appears in several places, it should stop being treated as one local annoyance and become a transversal organizational issue.

[Inferential] At that point, the question becomes whether the association needs reinforcement, binomes, or another safety measure. His role is then to justify the cost to the tarificateur in terms of transport quality, safety, and service reality.

## Product-framing implication

[Inferential] The stronger fit surface at AAJD is therefore not generic participation software. It is better described as infrastructure that helps plural field inputs become legible, aggregated, and decision-ready across dispositifs without removing human accountability.
